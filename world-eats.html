World food & street lunch

Side streets, food halls, and counters · Often £6–9 for a filling plate

The interesting cheap meals are rarely on the main tourist drag. Duck into Chinatown side streets for set lunches, follow lunchtime office workers to Korean or Japanese spots with tray meals, or grab bánh mì, falafel, or gözleme from small shops a block off Oxford Street or near Brick Lane. Large Asian supermarkets often have a hot food counter — unglamorous seating, but a full plate in minutes between museums.

How to keep the bill low

Counter service and takeaway skip table service and sometimes VAT quirks. “Set lunch” or “lunch menu” boards before 15:00–17:00 beat dinner prices. Split one large dish between two if you’re not starving — many traders portion generously.

What to watch for

Card readers may suggest a tip — tap “no” if you’re on a tight budget. Check whether service is included in sit-down places. At busy street windows, have cash as a backup; many places are card-only too.

Timing

Weekday lunch (12:00–14:00) is when set menus and office crowds keep kitchens busy and prices honest. Saturday markets are fun but crowded — go early or late to avoid the worst queues.
