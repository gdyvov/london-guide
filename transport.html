Oyster · contactless

Move for less

Oyster or contactless on TfL: same fares, automatic daily caps, Hopper buses, and a few rules that are easy to get right once you know them.

Fares that cap themselves

Transport for London (TfL) runs most of the Underground, DLR, Elizabeth line (within London), London Overground, London Trams, and buses on one pay-as-you-go scale when you touch in and out with Oyster or contactless (bank card or mobile wallet). Journeys add up until you hit a daily cap for the zone combination you actually used — after that, further rides that day in the same cap “bucket” are not charged extra (caps are updated each year; check the official TfL table before you travel). Paper Day Travelcards from machines usually cost more than letting contactless cap — use paper only if you have no tap card.

Short notes — tap to expand

Oyster card

Reusable blue card for TfL. Top up at machines, use PAYG fares and daily caps. Good if you want fewer foreign-card charges and possible Railcard discounts on eligible routes.

Contactless card or phone

Same fares and caps as Oyster. Register your card on TfL for journey history and refunds. Do not mix different cards/devices in one day or your cap may not combine correctly.

Zones, peak times, touch out

Zone 1-2 is most central London; Heathrow is zone 6. Peak weekday windows cost more on many rail journeys. Always touch out even if gates are open to avoid maximum fare charges.

Bus vs Tube

Buses are flat fare and scenic; Tube is faster for long hops. For short city centre moves, bus can be cheaper than multiple Tube changes.

Hopper fare

After first bus/tram tap, unlimited extra bus/tram rides within one hour are included. Hopper applies only to buses and trams, not Tube or rail.

Elizabeth line, DLR, Night Tube

Elizabeth line and DLR use Oyster/contactless inside London. Night Tube runs on selected lines Friday-Saturday nights; check station posters for current service and fare details.

River boats and hire bikes

River services and public hire bikes are separate products and usually do not count toward Tube/bus daily caps. Use apps for live boat times and bike dock availability.

Walking, interchanges & airport links

Many Tube “interchanges” are a long walk in a tunnel; sometimes leaving the station and walking 500 metres above ground saves time and a zone. Large rail termini (Paddington, King’s Cross, Victoria) connect to buses on the street — follow the blue “Buses” signs. For Gatwick and Stansted, national rail operators sell their own tickets; Oyster/contactless may only cover part of the route or not apply at all — read the orange route validators before you tap. When in doubt, ask a TfL staff member in a high-vis jacket at the gate line: wrong taps are easier to fix before you travel than after.
