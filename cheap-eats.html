Cafés, pubs & cheap plates

Sit-down rooms, caff fry-ups, pub kitchens, and food halls — the same city as the tourist menus, roughly half the price if you know where to look.

Eat well without the bill shock

Skip souvenir-strip cafés: neighbourhood speciality coffee shops and bakeries do drink-plus-pastry for less than hotel brunch. Classic caffs plate fry-ups and jacket potatoes for small change before noon. Pubs run daytime menus and lunch boards until mid-afternoon — often the cheapest sit-down dinner zone if you avoid premium ale houses. Add Chinatown set lunches, Korean or Japanese tray counters under £9, falafel and mezze shops, and salt-beef off Brick Lane. Food halls near stations bundle noodles, roti, and sushi without a booking fee. At markets, one hot dish plus a walk to the river beats a window-table surcharge. Tap water is free; in pubs, check if service is already on the receipt.

Cafés, pubs, counters & markets

Cafés & bakeries

Independent roasters and bakeries: flat white plus croissant or cardamom bun, often under a chain brunch. Follow the morning queue of locals.

under £5–7

Caffs & greasy spoons

Formica counters, builder’s tea, and full breakfasts at prices that haven’t heard of “small plates”. Order at the till; cash can still win when the card machine plays up.

fry-up

Pub kitchens & lunch boards

Burgers, pies, curry clubs, and chalkboard specials — pubs are some of London’s most honest cheap restaurants. Check kitchen hours; food service can end before the bar fills up.

sit-down

Food halls & counters

Station arches and office atriums: buzzer queues, benches, and visible portions — noodles, roti bowls, sushi sets, often £7–11 with no booking drama.

quick

World food & small restaurants

Bánh mì, bibimbap, gözleme, salt-beef bagels — side-street rooms and Asian supermarket food courts pack flavour for £6–9 away from the main drag.

global

Borough Market

Grills, cheese, bread, and traders from all over — pick one hot dish or a small plate to share, then walk five minutes to the Thames. Go Tuesday–Thursday to dodge the worst Saturday crush.
